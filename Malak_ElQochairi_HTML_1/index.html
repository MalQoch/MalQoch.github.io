Wide Sargasso Sea

Author: Jean Rhys

Genre: Historical Fiction

Year Released: 1966

Awards: W.H. Simth Literary Award (1967)
Fun Fact: This novel is a prequel to *Jane Eyre* by Charlotte Brontë.

Wide Sargasso Sea tells the untold story of the woman known as the “madwoman in the attic” from Jane Eyre, and gives her the voice that was stripped from her.
It explores identity, colonialism, madness, and the importance of perspective through vivid Caribbean imagery, and switching the narrator between the main characters throughout the novel.
Rhys gives a voice to a character previously silenced in classic literature by showcasing her previous life before become a "mad woman" and how her misfortunes led to her madness.
